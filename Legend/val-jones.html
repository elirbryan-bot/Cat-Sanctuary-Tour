Conner House

Val Jones named after Valerie Jones. Valerie helped rescue 15 feral cats from under the Arthur Laing bridge and became friends with the humans of the Sanctuary. Valerie Jones was affected by ALS (Lou Gehrig’s Disease), and was taken from us much too young. Val’s family came to visit the shelter the very next day. Subsequently they heard of various Sanctuary projects and decided that they wanted to donate the funds for the construction of what became known as the Val Jones area which is one of our FIV areas. These cats in here are also on a special diet so we can't have any treats in there. They're in a urinary diet that will help protects their bladders from inflammation, infection or urinary crystal. Everybody in here is very, very sweet. Because FIV is spread by fighting or mating, the majority of our FIV positive cats are male. We have very few females. Thankfully, once we neuter them, the incidence of fighting is greatly reduced.
